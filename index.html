Services

UX Design

I can help you improve the user experience of your app or website. I can aid you in structuring your site so the users don't get trapped in a corner, I can help you turn traffic into actual sales and I can help you create a lasting impression, that will improve your retention rates.

I work fluently in the field of Interaction- and User Experience Design, Information Architecture and Visual Design and I can handle the entire life-cycle of the design from the early lo-fi prototypes, to advanced hi-fi prototypes to actual 'production ready' web development.

Web Development

I always strive for a good consistent experience across browsers and devices. To achieve that I use responsive design and feature detection to achieve what is known as graceful degradation and progressive enhancement, making sure that both those with capable as well as less capable devices will get the best experience feasible to build. Everything is built in CSS3, HTML5 and Javascript and a lot of effort is put into keeping the footprint of the site as small and the graphics as crisp as possible.

I implement your new web design or a simple web apps in either a common and easy maintainable and extensible CMS, such as Wordpress, Joomla or Drupal, or if the task at hand requires it, I build it from scratch in a web framework such as Rails, CakePHP or Meteor.js.

Business Consultancy

Fourp.io

Fourp.io is an exciting new platform that aims to provide an alternative place to express opinion, which is much less "noisy" than the usual suspects of social media.

The intention of Fourp.io is to create an intimate experience which is similar to what is found when 4 people are sitting around a dinner-table. Fourp.io features a collaborative text-editor which allows to invite up to 3 friends to blog collaboratively and then publish to a community of other "fourpio'ers". Fourp.io is to be released in an early public beta very soon.

Music App "proof of concept"

This prototype was built as part of the Tigerspring-project above, but was never implemented. It is an entirely Photoshop-based mockup, designed to test some design ideas. The design is based on two always available "off-canvas" screens, the navigation and the actual player. Further, I am experimenting with presenting featured content in a horizontal scrolling panel. Aside from these experiments I strived to adhere to the iOS design guidelines as much as possible. It was designed weeks before iOS 7 and the major wave of flat design on iOS, though, which is part of the reason it was never implemented as-is.
